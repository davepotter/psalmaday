<h3>Psalm 11</h3>
<div class="container">
<div class="row">
<div class="number">1</div><div class="verse"> [For the Chief Musician. By David.] In Yahweh, I take refuge. How can you say to my soul, "Flee as a bird to your mountain!"</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">2</div><div class="verse"> For, behold, the wicked bend their bows. They set their arrows on the strings, that they may shoot in darkness at the upright in heart.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">3</div><div class="verse"> If the foundations are destroyed, what can the righteous do?</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">4</div><div class="verse"> Yahweh is in his holy temple. Yahweh is on his throne in heaven. His eyes observe. His eyes examine the children of men.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">5</div><div class="verse"> Yahweh examines the righteous, but the wicked and him who loves violence his soul hates.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">6</div><div class="verse"> On the wicked he will rain blazing coals; fire, sulfur, and scorching wind shall be the portion of their cup.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">7</div><div class="verse"> For Yahweh is righteous. He loves righteousness. The upright shall see his face.</div>
</div>
</div>
