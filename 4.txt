<h3>Psalm 4</h3>
<div class="container">
<div class="row">
<div class="number">1</div><div class="verse"> [For the Chief Musician; on stringed instruments. A Psalm by David.] Answer me when I call, God of my righteousness. Give me relief from my distress. Have mercy on me, and hear my prayer.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">2</div><div class="verse"> You sons of men, how long shall my glory be turned into dishonor? Will you love vanity, and seek after falsehood? Selah.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">3</div><div class="verse"> But know that Yahweh has set apart for himself him who is godly: Yahweh will hear when I call to him.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">4</div><div class="verse"> Stand in awe, and don't sin. Search your own heart on your bed, and be still. Selah.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">5</div><div class="verse"> Offer the sacrifices of righteousness. Put your trust in Yahweh.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">6</div><div class="verse"> Many say, "Who will show us any good?" Yahweh, let the light of your face shine on us.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">7</div><div class="verse"> You have put gladness in my heart, more than when their grain and their new wine are increased.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">8</div><div class="verse"> In peace I will both lay myself down and sleep, for you, Yahweh alone, make me live in safety.</div>
</div>
</div>
