<h3>Psalm 60</h3>
<div class="container">
<div class="row">
<div class="number">1</div><div class="verse"> [For the Chief Musician. To the tune of "The Lily of the Covenant." A teaching poem by David, when he fought with Aram Naharaim and with Aram Zobah, and Joab returned, and killed twelve thousand of Edom in the Valley of Salt.] God, you have rejected us. You have broken us down. You have been angry. Restore us, again.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">2</div><div class="verse"> You have made the land tremble. You have torn it. Mend its fractures, for it quakes.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">3</div><div class="verse"> You have shown your people hard things. You have made us drink the wine that makes us stagger.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">4</div><div class="verse"> You have given a banner to those who fear you, that it may be displayed because of the truth. Selah.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">5</div><div class="verse"> So that your beloved may be delivered, save with your right hand, and answer us.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">6</div><div class="verse"> God has spoken from his sanctuary: "I will triumph. I will divide Shechem, and measure out the valley of Succoth.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">7</div><div class="verse"> Gilead is mine, and Manasseh is mine. Ephraim also is the defense of my head. Judah is my scepter.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">8</div><div class="verse"> Moab is my wash basin. I will throw my shoe on Edom. I shout in triumph over Philistia."</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">9</div><div class="verse"> Who will bring me into the strong city? Who has led me to Edom?</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">10</div><div class="verse"> Haven't you, God, rejected us? You don't go out with our armies, God.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">11</div><div class="verse"> Give us help against the adversary, for the help of man is vain.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">12</div><div class="verse"> Through God we shall do valiantly, for it is he who will tread down our adversaries.</div>
</div>
</div>
