<h3>Psalm 58</h3>
<div class="container">
<div class="row">
<div class="number">1</div><div class="verse"> [For the Chief Musician. To the tune of "Do Not Destroy." A poem by David.] Do you indeed speak righteousness, silent ones? Do you judge blamelessly, you sons of men?</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">2</div><div class="verse"> No, in your heart you plot injustice. You measure out the violence of your hands in the earth.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">3</div><div class="verse"> The wicked go astray from the womb. They are wayward as soon as they are born, speaking lies.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">4</div><div class="verse"> Their poison is like the poison of a snake; like a deaf cobra that stops its ear,</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">5</div><div class="verse"> which doesn't listen to the voice of charmers, no matter how skillful the charmer may be.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">6</div><div class="verse"> Break their teeth, God, in their mouth. Break out the great teeth of the young lions, Yahweh.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">7</div><div class="verse"> Let them vanish as water that flows away. When they draw the bow, let their arrows be made blunt.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">8</div><div class="verse"> Let them be like a snail which melts and passes away, like the stillborn child, who has not seen the sun.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">9</div><div class="verse"> Before your pots can feel the heat of the thorns, he will sweep away the green and the burning alike.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">10</div><div class="verse"> The righteous shall rejoice when he sees the vengeance. He shall wash his feet in the blood of the wicked;</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">11</div><div class="verse"> so that men shall say, "Most certainly there is a reward for the righteous. Most certainly there is a God who judges the earth."</div>
</div>
</div>
