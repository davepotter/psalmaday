<h3>Psalm 77</h3>
<div class="container">
<div class="row">
<div class="number">1</div><div class="verse"> [For the Chief Musician. To Jeduthun. A Psalm by Asaph.] My cry goes to God! Indeed, I cry to God for help, and for him to listen to me.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">2</div><div class="verse"> In the day of my trouble I sought the Lord. My hand was stretched out in the night, and didn't get tired. My soul refused to be comforted.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">3</div><div class="verse"> I remember God, and I groan. I complain, and my spirit is overwhelmed. Selah.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">4</div><div class="verse"> You hold my eyelids open. I am so troubled that I can't speak.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">5</div><div class="verse"> I have considered the days of old, the years of ancient times.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">6</div><div class="verse"> I remember my song in the night. I consider in my own heart; my spirit diligently inquires:</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">7</div><div class="verse"> "Will the Lord reject us forever? Will he be favorable no more?</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">8</div><div class="verse"> Has his loving kindness vanished forever? Does his promise fail for generations?</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">9</div><div class="verse"> Has God forgotten to be gracious? Has he, in anger, withheld his compassion?" Selah.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">10</div><div class="verse"> Then I thought, "I will appeal to this: the years of the right hand of the Most High."</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">11</div><div class="verse"> I will remember Yah's deeds; for I will remember your wonders of old.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">12</div><div class="verse"> I will also meditate on all your work, and consider your doings.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">13</div><div class="verse"> Your way, God, is in the sanctuary. What god is great like God?</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">14</div><div class="verse"> You are the God who does wonders. You have made your strength known among the peoples.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">15</div><div class="verse"> You have redeemed your people with your arm, the sons of Jacob and Joseph. Selah.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">16</div><div class="verse"> The waters saw you, God. The waters saw you, and they writhed. The depths also convulsed.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">17</div><div class="verse"> The clouds poured out water. The skies resounded with thunder. Your arrows also flashed around.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">18</div><div class="verse"> The voice of your thunder was in the whirlwind. The lightnings lit up the world. The earth trembled and shook.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">19</div><div class="verse"> Your way was through the sea; your paths through the great waters. Your footsteps were not known.</div>
</div>
<div class="row">
<div class="number">20</div><div class="verse"> You led your people like a flock, by the hand of Moses and Aaron.</div>
</div>
</div>
